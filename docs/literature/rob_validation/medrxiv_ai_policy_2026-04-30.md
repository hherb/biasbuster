# medRxiv generative AI policy — verbatim quote

**Source:** medRxiv FAQ, https://www.medrxiv.org/about/FAQ
**Retrieved:** 2026-04-30
**Section heading on page:** *"How should I acknowledge the use of generative AI for writing my preprint?"*

## Verbatim text

> Authors are responsible for the content they submit, including material produced by generative AI and related technologies. As with other computational methodologies, use of these tools should be detailed in manuscripts, and the output must not plagiarize other work or breach copyright. In any case, authors are accountable for the accuracy, integrity, and originality of their work. AI tools and large language models (LLMs) do not meet this requirement and should not be listed as authors on articles.

## Operational implications for our work

1. **AI cannot be listed as an author** on a medRxiv preprint. Period.
2. **AI use must be detailed** in the manuscript ("As with other computational methodologies").
3. **Authors retain accountability** for accuracy, integrity, and originality.
4. **Output must not plagiarize or breach copyright** — relevant to any AI-generated prose.

## Cross-reference

This policy aligns with ICMJE's authorship criteria (which require accountability for the work) and the Springer Nature / Science / Nature group policies finalized in 2023–2024. The principled rationale is identical: an LLM cannot be held accountable for the integrity of a defined contribution after publication, which is a non-negotiable element of authorship.
